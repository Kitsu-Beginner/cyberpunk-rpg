Neural Network & Hacking Protocols

In the year 2050, the battlefield is digital as often as it is physical. Attacks occur on two vectors: Wireless Injection (remote takeover) and Direct Link (physical port access, no rules as of yet). To hack a target wirelessly, the target and the attacker must be in each others range.

Signal & Range Logic

  • Handshake Protocol: A connection can only be established within the lowest range of the two devices. (e.g., If Device A has 5km range and Device B has 10km, connection is only possible within 5km).
  • Urban Interference: In dense city environments, all signal ranges are effective halved due to structural interference.
  • Visibility: Devices within range are not immediately visible; they must be identified via a Scan program.
  • Micro-Devices: Purely wireless devices often have ranges of only a few meters. These are usually hacked via their host (e.g., the owner's Commlink).

Base Stats

Compute (CPU)

Determines the processing power available for offensive and utility programs.

  • Each active program consumes 1 Compute per level.
  • The Program Level is added to your dice pool for attacks.
  • Cyberdecks (Mobile Quantum Computers) offer significantly higher Compute than standard Commlinks.

Firewall (FWL)

The primary defense rating against intrusion. It acts as the Threshold (Difficulty) for hacking attempts.

Dynamic Defense Rule:
Any unused Compute capacity is automatically rerouted to the Firewall.
Example: A Commlink with Compute 2 and Firewall 2 running no programs has an Effective Firewall of 4.

Attack Vectors

Hacking requires a dedicated Cyberdeck with a Hacking OS. Commlinks can run defense and utility, but cannot launch intrusion attacks. The Hacker rolls an attack roll and usually needs to meet (firewall) successes to succeed. The effect is defined by the program that was used in the attack.

Program Library

1. Network Scan REQ: Cyberdeck (Hacking_OS)
Scans the environment for wireless signatures. The hacker perceives (used Compute) devices with a Firewall rating ≤ Successes rolled. If more devices are present, than the used compute, the hacker has to either perform multiple scans or limit the search to only match vertain criteria.

Effect: Reveals Device Type, Stats, and approximate distance (±1km).
2. Gain Access (Root) REQ: Cyberdeck (Hacking OS) | Target: Scan Lock
Attempts to establish a backdoor for passive monitoring.
Threshold: Target Firewall.

Success: Access to GPS data, chat logs, media files, and contact lists.
Failure: Target receives a security log notification (often delayed/unnoticed until later analysis).
3. Gain Access (Admin) REQ: Cyberdeck (Hacking OS) | Target: Root Access
Escalates privileges to seize full control of the device.
Threshold: Target Firewall.

Success: Complete device takeover (Shutdown, Reboot, Format, Control).
Failure: Active Lockout. The hacker is dumped from the system. No further attempts can be made on this device until the security resets.
4. System Overload (Slow Down) REQ: Cyberdeck (Hacking OS) | Target: Scan Lock
Floods the target with garbage requests to freeze their processing power. Does not require Access privileges.

Effect: Attacker makes an opposed roll against the targets firewall. The target device looses 1 compute for each net hit on the opposed roll as long as the programm is active..
Note: The attacker's Compute used for this attack remains "occupied" as long as the jammer is active.
5. Tactical Network (TacNet) REQ: Any device | Cost: 1 Compute
Links team commlinks to share real-time telemetry: GPS, Ammo counts, Biomonitor status, and Enemy sightlines. An AI predicts enemy movement based on shared vision. Drones can also be connected to the network.

Bonus: Flank Immunity. Team members cannot be flanked (which usually halves defense pools) as long as at least one linked member has Line of Sight to the attacker. If an attacker breaches on of the linked devices in such a network, he can readout all transmitted data of all members in the network.
6. Drone Command REQ: Any Device | Cost: Variable
Allows the user to issue commands to linked drones or assume direct control.

Cost: Occupies 1 Compute slot per active drone in the network.
